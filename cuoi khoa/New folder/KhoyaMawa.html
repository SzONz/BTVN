Direction

  • Take a heavy-bottomed pan or a non-stick pan and pour 1 liter of full-fat milk into it. Use a pan with a larger surface area to speed up the process.
  • Place the pan over medium heat and bring the milk to a boil. Stir it occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  • Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer. Keep stirring the milk at regular intervals to avoid burning and forming a skin on top.
  • Continue simmering and stirring until the milk thickens and reduces significantly. This process can take anywhere from 1 to 2 hours or even longer, depending on the quantity of milk and the desired consistency.
  • As the milk reduces, it will start to change color, turning from white to a creamy, pale yellow. The texture will become thicker and grainy.
  • The khoya is ready when it becomes a thick, dough-like consistency and starts to leave the sides of the pan. You should be able to roll it into a ball without it sticking to your hands.
  • Once the khoya reaches the desired consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  • You can use the khoya immediately in your favorite Indian sweets or dishes. If you want to store it, let it cool completely, shape it into blocks or portions, and store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Khoya can be kept in the refrigerator for a few days and can also be frozen for longer storage.
